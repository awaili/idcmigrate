Connection model (how executors talk to idc-migrate)

      
Executors connect to idc-migrate — they poll POST /api/executor/tasks/claim and push results back over the internet to this server's public URL (IDC_PUBLIC_URL). idc-migrate never initiates a connection to any executor, so an executor may sit behind NAT/firewall with only outbound access. The connected dot means the executor polled idc-migrate recently (claim/lease/complete), not that idc-migrate reached it — there is no Test button because idc-migrate has nothing to reach out with.

      
A trigger targets one executor (pick per trigger on the Scan & Migrate page): empty = the pool (any approved executor may claim it), or a named id = target that executor. Named executors self-register: an executor calls POST /api/executors/register (optionally gated by IDC_ENROLL_SECRET), lands pending with no token, and you Approve it here — idc-migrate then mints its token and shows it once for you to relay out-of-band. The default executor is the single operator-configured one (IDC_EXECUTOR_TOKEN). Push-back auth accepts any approved executor's token.
